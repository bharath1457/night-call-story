“Some nights don’t need moonlight—just a familiar voice in the dark.”

The Night Call

Bunny was lying on his bed in his room, lights switched off, phone glowing softly in the dark. He couldn’t sleep. His thoughts kept circling back to one person—Sriya.

He pressed call. After a few rings, she answered in a hushed voice, “Hello Bunny… everyone’s asleep here in hostel, I had to cover my face with a blanket to talk.”

Bunny smiled instantly. “And here I am, alone in my room, waiting just to hear your voice. You know, this silence feels heavy until you break it.”

Sriya giggled quietly. “You and your lines… but still, I like them.”

There was a pause. Both listened to each other’s breathing. Bunny softly said, “Sriya… my room feels empty without you. I keep looking at my study table, wishing you were here teasing me to focus.”

Her tone softened. “And my hostel bed feels cold, Bunny… but when you talk, it feels like I’m back home, safe.”

They spoke about random things—college jokes, their future plans, even silly fights they had in class. Slowly the time passed, but neither wanted to hang up.

As her voice grew sleepy, she whispered, “Promise me Bunny… you’ll never stop calling me at night.”

He closed his eyes, smiling in the dark. “I promise, Sriya. Goodnight… dream of me.”

Her last sleepy “hmm” stayed in his ears long after the call ended. Bunny kept staring at the phone screen, his heart full, before finally falling asleep.
